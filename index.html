DeFi Security - MEV/BEV MEV (Miner/Maximum Extracted Value) and BEV (Blockchain Extracted Value) refer to any value that is extracted or extractable from a blockchain through the actions of miners or other blockchain participants. It can be challenging to determine whether a particular MEV/BEV opportunity is an attack or not, as there are instances where MEV/BEV opportunities are not attacks (e.g., arbitrage) and instances where attacks are not MEV/BEV (e.g., compromise of a wallet private key). In other words, MEV/BEV and attacks are not necessarily mutually exclusive.

MEV/BEV risks refer to the potential for miners or other blockchain participants to extract value from a blockchain in a way that may compromise the security and integrity of the network. This can include front-running, where a miner takes advantage of advanced knowledge of a pending transaction or order to execute a trade that profits from that knowledge, and reorg attacks, where the history of a blockchain is altered by replacing a portion of the chain with a different set of blocks. Both of these risks can lead to losses for traders and users who rely on the integrity of the blockchain for their transactions, and can be difficult to detect and prevent. To mitigate these risks, it is important for DeFi systems to have strong security measures in place, such as robust consensus algorithms and effective measures for detecting and preventing malicious activity.
